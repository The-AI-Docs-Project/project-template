# ADP (AI Docs Project) — Community Code of Conduct

## Introduction
As contributors, maintainers, and participants in the **AI Docs Project (ADP)**, we are committed to fostering an open, inclusive, and welcoming community. Our goal is to create a collaborative environment where contributors can improve technical documentation, share knowledge, and grow professionally.

We pledge to respect all individuals, regardless of age, experience level, ethnicity, gender, gender identity, sexual orientation, disability, or any other aspect of identity.  



## Scope
This Code of Conduct applies to:

- All ADP repositories, documentation, forums, and communication channels (e.g., GitHub, Discord, mailing lists)  
- Interactions in community spaces, including pull requests, issues, comments, and documentation reviews  
- Any event or activity where participants are representing ADP  


## Our Standards

### Examples of positive behavior
We value contributions that build a supportive, collaborative, and professional community. Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- Writing clear, structured, and user-focused documentation  
- Asking thoughtful questions and giving actionable feedback  
- Respecting differing opinions, experiences, and technical skill levels  
- Helping new contributors understand documentation processes  
- Explaining major changes with reasoning (e.g., usability, clarity, readability)  
- Prioritizing **end-user understanding** over personal preference  

### Examples of unacceptable behavior
The following behaviors are considered unacceptable:

- Dismissing contributions or feedback without explanation  
- Hostile, sarcastic, or demeaning communication  
- Personal attacks or blaming individuals instead of addressing content  
- Overruling or rewriting contributions without discussion or reasoning  
- Harassment, discrimination, or exclusion of any kind  
- Publicly shaming contributors for mistakes or differing opinions  

### Documentation-specific expectations
Given ADP’s focus on technical documentation:

- Contributors should focus on **clarity, accessibility, and usability**  
- Feedback should be **constructive and actionable**, not judgmental  
- Discussions should remain focused on improving documentation, not proving expertise  



## Maintainer Responsibilities
Maintainers are responsible for:

- Reviewing contributions fairly and consistently  
- Providing clear explanations for approvals or rejections  
- Guiding contributors toward best practices in documentation  
- Removing or editing contributions that violate this Code of Conduct  
- Ensuring that the community remains welcoming and inclusive  


## Reporting Violations
If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code of Conduct:

- Contact the ADP maintainers via **[contactadp@zohomail.com](mailto:contactadp@zohomail.com)**  
- Include context such as links, screenshots, or descriptions of the incident  

All reports will be handled **confidentially, respectfully, and promptly**. Retaliation against anyone reporting a violation is prohibited.



## Enforcement
Actions taken in response to violations may include:

- Requesting clarification or correction  
- Removing or editing contributions  
- Temporary or permanent restriction from project spaces  

Decisions will be based on what best protects the community and fosters a safe, productive environment.
